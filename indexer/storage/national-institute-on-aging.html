About us

The National Institute on Aging (NIA), part of the National Institutes of Health, is the U.S. Government's lead agency on aging and Alzheimer's disease and related dementias research. You can also connect with @NIHAging on Twitter, Facebook, and YouTube.

    One of the trendier topics in science and medicine today is machine learning, a type of artificial intelligence. For machine learning, researchers provide massive amounts of data into a workflow to discover emerging disease patterns. The amount of data produced across many scientific disciplines creates a tremendous opportunity to accelerate insights into disease mechanisms. However, just one individual can produce a terabyte or a trillion bytes of DNA genetic sequence data. When you add to that other omics data, you quickly get an unwieldy amount of information. How do you decide what patterns to look for? Enter GenoML, an open-source project created by CARD staff and collaborators that offers a streamlined approach to machine learning in genomics. Learn more about GenoML here: https://lnkd.in/eaSqtjGN
